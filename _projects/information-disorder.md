## About this project

This project investigates how mis- and disinformation circulate, gain
traction, and resist moderation on social media platforms, with particular
attention to far-right communities and to periods of social and political
stress that act as natural experiments in information disorder — such as the
2019–2020 Australian bushfires and the COVID-19 pandemic.

Drawing on computational social science methods — network analysis,
mixed-methods classification, and predictive modelling — the work asks how
fringe actors move from the periphery to the centre of public conversations,
how platform moderation strategies succeed or fail, and how the early spread
of harmful content can be detected before it scales.
