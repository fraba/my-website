## About this project

This project investigates how far-right communities operate in online
platforms and how mis- and disinformation circulate, gain traction, and
resist moderation during periods of social and political stress.

Drawing on computational social science methods — network analysis,
mixed-methods classification, and predictive modelling — the work examines
events such as the 2019–2020 Australian bushfires and the COVID-19 pandemic
as natural experiments in information disorder. It asks how fringe actors
move from the periphery to the centre of public conversations, how platform
moderation strategies succeed or fail, and how the early spread of harmful
content can be detected before it scales.
